# Current Approach: The "Pillar" Metabolic Enhancement Framework
**Modified:** 2026-04-06T20:38:51+07:00

**Status:** ACTIVE
**Core Philosophy:** In Vivo Functional Selection > In Silico Prediction.

## 1. Executive Summary
Project Garlic has transitioned from a purely theoretical computational model to a **Phenotypic-First** isolation strategy. We hypothesize that by selecting bacteria that survival-flourish in a sulfur-stressed environment (The "Pillar" Medium), we can bypass expensive genomic sequencing while ensuring the isolated strains possess the high-efficacy metabolic machinery needed to upregulate garlic's defensive and quality pathways.

## 2. Strategic Pillars

### A. Functional Isolation (Bioinformatics "Salvage")
*   **The Problem:** De novo sequencing of local soil microbiomes is cost-prohibitive.
*   **The Solution:** Use **Differential Media** as a localized biological "filter." 
*   **Mechanism:** Bacteria are isolated based on their ability to solubilize inorganic sulfur and survive selective pressures. This phenotypic response serves as a direct proxy for the presence of the `cys` and `ssu` gene clusters.

### B. Synergistic Validation (2x2 Factorial Design)
To prove the efficacy of the "Bio-Targeted" approach, we utilize a four-quadrant experimental model:
1.  **Baseline (Negative Control):** Establishes the natural growth rate.
2.  **Biological Power (Bio-Only):** Measures the standalone metabolic boost from our isolate.
3.  **Chemical Standard (Fert-Only):** The benchmark for current agricultural practices.
4.  **The Resonant Effect (Combined):** Our primary goal—testing if the microbes enhance the nutrient-use efficiency of standard fertilizers.

## 3. Target Quality Matrix ("Golden Indices")
Our success is measured by the **Total Quality** index, rather than raw biomass:
*   **TPC (Total Phenolic Content):** The primary indicator of antioxidant value.
*   **Brix (TSS):** The index for bulb density, flavor, and storage resilience.
*   **Allicin Efficiency:** The secondary medicinal marker.
