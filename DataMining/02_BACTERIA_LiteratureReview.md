# Candidate Bacteria Literature Review

**Focus:** Evaluation of bacterial candidates for the "Total Package" strain (S-mobilization, Stress Defense, Growth Promotion).

---

# 1. Bacillus

## 1.1. Bacillus megaterium

### **1.1.1. [Saeid et al., 2018] [Phosphorus Solubilization by Bacillus Species](https://doi.org/10.3390/molecules23112897)**
**Source**: *Molecules* (2018)

**High Efficiency P-Solubilization**
> "The combination of Bacillus megaterium with the fish bones at 5 g/L is proposed as the pair that gives the highest concentration of released phosphorus (483 ± 5 mg/L)."

> "The main mechanism recognized to be responsible for the solubilization of phosphorus is the production of different types of organic acids."

### **1.1.2. [Santos et al., 2024] [Inoculation with Bacillus megaterium CNPMS B119 and Bacillus subtilis CNPMS B2084 improve P-acquisition and maize yield in Brazil](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/microbiology/articles/10.3389/fmicb.2024.1426166/full)**
**Source**: *Frontiers in Microbiology* (2024)

**Mechanisms (Strain B119)**
> "Both the Bacillus strains exhibited plant growth-promoting properties relevant to P dynamics, including phosphate solubilization and mineralization."

> "B2084 was highly effective in solubilizing calcium phosphate but had a limited capacity for dissolving iron phosphate compared to strain B119 [which was more effective for Fe-P]."

**IAA Production**
> "Both Bacillus strains produce high levels of the phytohormone IAA-like, particularly B119, known to stimulate root growth."

## 1.2. Bacillus subtilis

### **1.2.1. [Santos et al., 2024] [Inoculation with Bacillus megaterium CNPMS B119 and Bacillus subtilis CNPMS B2084 improve P-acquisition and maize yield in Brazil](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/microbiology/articles/10.3389/fmicb.2024.1426166/full)**
**Source**: *Frontiers in Microbiology* (2024)

**Biofilm & Root Adhesion (Strain B2084)**
> "The biofilm from strain B2084 covered most of the tested root, while the biofilm from strain B119 appeared more irregular."

> "It is likely that the ability of strains B119 and B2084 to adhere to maize roots was facilitated by their production of EPS and biofilms, which serve as the primary mode of adhesion for gram-positive bacteria to roots."

**P-Solubilization**
> "B2084 was highly effective in solubilizing calcium phosphate."

### **1.2.2. [Nayanashree et al., 2020] [Biodegradation of Vulcanized SBR: A Comparison between Bacillus subtilis, Pseudomonas aeruginosa and Streptomyces sp](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-019-55530-y)**
**Source**: *Scientific Reports* (2020)

**Sulfur Devulcanization**
> "B. subtilis, P. aeruginosa, and Streptomyces sp. evoked after 4 weeks a loss in v-SBR crosslinks by 17.15, 10.68 and 43.39%."

> "FTIR findings indicate that the polymeric chain has been partially consumed causing C-C bonds scission indicating the biodegradation and bio-devulcanization phenomena."

**Biodegradation Efficiency**
> "The bacterial strains caused a carbon loss by... 16.09%... after four weeks for B. subtilis."

## 1.3. Bacillus cereus

### **1.3.1. [Kashif et al., 2022] [Whole-Genome and Transcriptome Sequencing-Based Characterization of Bacillus Cereus NR1 From Subtropical Marine Mangrove and Its Potential Role in Sulfur Metabolism](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/microbiology/articles/10.3389/fmicb.2022.856092/full)**
**Source**: *Frontiers in Microbiology* (2022)

**Genomic Sulfur Capability**
> "Furthermore, 17 sulfur metabolism-related genes, including genes involved in assimilation sulfate reduction, APS and PAPS, and the cys, ssu, and TST gene families, were identified."

> "A relatively complete assimilated sulfate reduction pathway and the cysteine biosynthesis pathway were found through whole-genome sequencing."

**Desulfurization Efficiency**
> "Our findings showed that NR1 desulfurized DBT via the 4S pathway at a 90% of desulfurization rate."

> "In NR1, this sulfur metabolism pathway may be widely present in Bacillus species and is capable of sulfur metabolism, thereby predicting the high sulfur utilization capacity."

## 1.4. Bacillus safensis

### **1.4.1. [Numan et al., 2018] [Efficacy of Indole Acetic Acid and Exopolysaccharides-Producing Bacillus safensis Strain FN13 for Inducing Cd-Stress Tolerance and Plant Growth Promotion in Brassica juncea (L.)](https://www.mdpi.com/2076-3417/11/9/4160)**
**Source**: *Applied Sciences* (2021)

**Stress Tolerance (Cd) & EPS**
> "Bacillus safensis strain FN13 was the most effective strain in improving the Brassica juncea (L.) growth and physiology under Cd-stress."

> "The Cd-tolerant PGPR strains help to reduce Cd uptake of crop plants by stabilizing it in the soil through production of exopolysaccharides."

**Growth Promotion**
> "Phytohormone (IAA) and exopolysaccharides (EPS) producing plant growth-promoting rhizobacteria can reduce the toxicity of metals by stabilizing them in soil."
