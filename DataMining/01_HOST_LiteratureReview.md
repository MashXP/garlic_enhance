## 2. Heat Shock Response (HSP & HSF)

> "A total of 114 HSP20 genes were identified in the garlic genome and designated as AsHSP20-1 to AsHSP20-114 according to their chromosomal positions (Table 1)."

Conversely, AsHSP20-38 and -20 displayed progressive downregulation over the treatment period, indicating that these genes may be repressed by high temperature or function predominantly under non-stress conditions."

> "Under salt stress (Figure 7B), AsHSP20-94, -81, -93, -79, -100, -95, -64, -68, -72, -38, -20 and -26 showed significant upregulation, with AsHSP20-79 and -94 exhibiting the most pronounced induction at 24 h."

**Functional Characterization**
> "Under salt treatment ([Figure 7](https://www.mdpi.com/2079-7737/14/10/1326#fig_body_display_biology-14-01326-f007)B), AsHSP20-94, -81, -93 and -79 again showed strong induction at 24 h, with AsHSP20-79 reaching the greatest expression level. Following MeJA application ([Figure 7](https://www.mdpi.com/2079-7737/14/10/1326#fig_body_display_biology-14-01326-f007)C), AsHSP20-94, -79 and -64 were markedly upregulated at 24 h, with AsHSP20-64 displaying the most pronounced response."

> "In contrast, yeast cells overexpressing AsHSP20-79 maintained robust growth across all tested temperatures, including 39 °C and 41 °C, demonstrating a marked enhancement in thermotolerance."

> "most selected AsHSP20 genes are rapidly and robustly induced by heat, salt and MeJA, with AsHSP20-94 and -79 emerging as particularly versatile multi-stress markers. Meanwhile, the downregulated subset may participate in energy reallocation or negative feedback during stress responses."
