## Phase 1: Differential Isolation & Screening (The "Salvage" Strategy)
*Instead of expensive sequencing, we use the environment as a filter for high-efficacy strains.*

### 1.1. The "Pillar" Hypothesis
*   **Premise:** Bacteria that thrive in a high-sulfur, low-nutrient environment are naturally equipped with the enzymatic machinery (`ssu`, `cys` genes) we need to enhance garlic.
*   **Action:** We will isolate local *Bacillus* strains that show the strongest phenotypic response on selective media.

### 1.2. Rough Isolation Ideas (For Phát)
1.  **Selective Sulfur Source:** Use Magnesium Sulfate ($MgSO_4$) or Ammonium Sulfate ($(NH_4)_2SO_4$) as the primary sulfur source in the "Pillar" medium.
2.  **Solubilization Indicator:** Incorporate **Bromocresol Purple** or **Bromocresol Blue**. A color change (acidification) around a colony indicates active metabolite secretion and sulfur processing.
3.  **Antifungal Barrier:** Add a selective antifungal agent (e.g., Nystatin) to ensure pure bacterial cultures from soil samples.
4.  **Desired Phenotype:** Prioritize large, fast-growing colonies with clear "halos" of acidification (> 5mm zone of inhibition/color shift).

### 1.3. Success Proxy & Measurement
*   **Colony Forming Units (CFU):** Target a count of $10^8$ CFU/mL for the final inoculant.
*   **Phenotypic Validation:** If the isolated strain achieves > 2 phenotypic markers (e.g., acidification AND biofilm formation), it is moved to Phase 2.

## Phase 2: Experimental Setup (2x2 Factorial Design)
*A robust 4-group model to test the synergy between biology and chemistry.*

### 2.1. Group Definitions (n=10 per group, Total n=40)
| Group ID | Treatment | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Control (C-)** | **Water Only** | Baseline growth in sterile substrate. |
| **Bio-Only (T1)** | **Microbes (+)** | Testing the standalone power of the isolated strain. |
| **Fert-Only (T2)** | **NPK (+)** | Standard chemical farming (100-150 kg N/ha equivalent). |
| **Combined (T3)** | **Both (+)** | Testing for synergistic enhancement of "Golden Indices." |

### 2.2. Planting Parameters (Research-Based)
*   **Variety:** Vietnamese Garlic (Ly Son/Hai Duong).
*   **Nitrogen (N) Rate:** 1.5g to 2.2g of Urea per 5L-10L pot (Targeting 100-150 kg N/ha field equivalent).
*   **Recommended Formula:** If possible, use a **18-10-14** NPK ratio supplemented with **S, Mn, Fe, and Zn** for the "Plus" groups (T2/T3).
*   **Timing:** 
    *   **Basal:** 33% N at planting.
    *   **Top-Dressing:** Split the remaining 67% into two doses (4 Weeks and 8 Weeks post-emergence).
    *   **The "Stop" Rule:** Cease all Nitrogen 4 weeks before target harvest to prioritize bulb swelling.
*   **Inoculation:** 30-minute clove soak in the isolation medium (T1 and T3 only).

## Phase 3: "Golden Indices" Measurement
*If Allicin fluctuates, these metrics ensure a successful scientific presentation.*

### 3.1. Non-Destructive (Growth)
*   Plant Height, Leaf Count, and Chlorophyll index (SPAD).

### 3.2. Destructive (Quality) - At Harvest
1.  **Total Phenolic Content (TPC):** Folin-Ciocalteu Assay. Proves antioxidant capacity.
2.  **Total Soluble Solids (TSS/Brix):** Handheld Refractometer. Proves bulb density and sugars.
3.  **Allicin Efficiency:** DTNB Spectrophotometry at 412nm.

---

## Phase 4: Data Correlation & Synergy Analysis
*   **The Synergy Index (SI):** Calculate if $T3 > (T1 + T2 - C-)$. If so, we've statistically proven a **positive synergistic effect** between the bio-targeted microbes and chemical fertilization.
*   **Nutrient Use Efficiency (NUE):** Compare T1 (Bio-only) with T2 (Fert-only). If the quality metrics (Brix/TPC) are comparable despite T1 having $0$ synthetic N, we've proven our isolation strategy can replace high-chemical dependency.
*   **The "Lead's" Statement (Phong):** "Our isolation strategy successfully identified a strain that not only survives but actively enhances the metabolic efficiency of the garlic crop, providing a 1+1 > 2 result."
