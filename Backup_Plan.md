# The "Safety Net" Protocol (Backup Plan)
**Modified:** 2026-04-06T20:38:51+07:00

**Objective:** To ensure academic success and project value *regardless* of whether the wet-lab experiment succeeds or fails.

---

## 1. Core Philosophy: "The Data is the Product, Not the Garlic"
*   **Shift Mindset:** You are not selling "super garlic." You are selling *knowledge* about how garlic interacts with endophytes.
*   **The Golden Rule:** A negative result (e.g., "Bacteria X had no effect") is scientifically valid *if* the methodology is rigorous. It saves future researchers from making the same mistake.
*   **Your Safety Net:** Your grade/evaluation depends on the *quality of your scientific process*, not the size of the garlic bulb.

## 2. The "Pre-Mortem" (Anticipating Failure)
*Ask these questions NOW, not at the end.*

| **Scenario** | **The "Panic" Reaction** | **The "Defensive Protocol" Response** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Garlic Dies** | "We failed. No data." | "We discovered a cytotoxicity threshold. Report focuses on 'Bacterial Compatibility Limits in *Allium*'." |
| **No Growth Change** | "The bacteria didn't work." | "We established a baseline. Use the Bioinfo model to explain *why* it might have failed (e.g., missing soil precursors)." |
| **Contamination** | "Throw it away." | "Pivot to identifying the contaminant. Is it an opportunist? That's an ecological finding." |

## 3. The Bioinformatics "Fortress" (Protecting Your Contribution)
*Since you fear the wet lab might fail, you must decouple your contribution from the physical plants.*

*   **Strategy:** Your work must stand alone.
    *   **Independent Deliverable 1:** A comprehensive *In Silico* review of Sulfur-metabolism gene networks in Garlic. (This is valuable even if you never touch a real plant).
    *   **Independent Deliverable 2:** A predictive model. "We *predicted* X. The wet lab showed Y. The discrepancy suggests factor Z." -> This discussion is often *more* impressive than a perfect match.
    *   **Benefit:** If the lab team (Phong/Phát) fails to grow the garlic, **YOU** still have a full computational report to present.
    *   **Action Plan:** If the pot trial fails (e.g., fungal infection), quickly pivot to the "In Silico Mock-up" data for the final presentation.

## 4. Checkpoints & "Kill Switches"
*Don't wait until the deadline to realize it's over.*

*   **Week 2 Checkpoint:** Are the bacteria alive?
    *   *No?* -> **Kill Switch:** Switch to a pure "Review & Theoretical Design" paper. Stop wasting resources.
*   **Week 4 Checkpoint:** Is the garlic growing?
    *   *No?* -> **Kill Switch:** Pivot to analyzing the "Stress Response." Measure stress markers instead of growth.

## 5. Team Communication
*   **Manage Expectations:** Tell the team: "There is a 50% chance this biology won't work. We need to document *every* step so we can write a 'Lessons Learned' paper if it fails."
*   **Your Role:** You are the "risk manager." while they focus on growing, you focus on *what implies* if the growing fails.
