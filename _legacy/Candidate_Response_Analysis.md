## 2. Analysis: "The Idea is Good, But..."

### ✅ The Good (Why it's tempting)
*   **Narrative Power:** "Zero Waste" and "Circular Economy" are excellent keywords for grants/competitions (SV-NCKH).
*   **Market Logic:** Garlic skins *are* rich in antioxidants, so the cosmetic angle is scientifically valid.
*   **Ambition:** She is thinking beyond the lab bench.

### 🚩 The Concerning (Why it's dangerous)
*   **Task Evasion:** She sidestepped the specific technical test (solvents/temps) to propose a role she finds more comfortable.
*   **Resource Drain:** Her offer to "learn from Nghĩa and Phát" defeats the purpose of hiring. We need a *Producer*, not a *Student*.
*   **Premature Optimization:** Planning bioplastics (Phase 3) before we have even grown the garlic (Phase 1) is a distraction.

---

## 3. The New Strategy: The "Yes, And..." Reframing
We will not reject her, nor will we let her be a "Strategy-only" member. We will anchor her high-level vision to our immediate low-level needs.

### Option D: The "Green Extraction & Valorization" Role (Recommended)
**The Hook:**
*   Accept her vision: "We love the Cosmetic/Zero-Waste idea."
*   **The Constraint:** "To make cosmetic ingredients, we cannot use rough lab solvents (Methanol). We need a **Green Extraction Protocol** (Safe Ethanol/Water) that is non-toxic."

**The Deal:**
*   **She must:** Develop the "Cosmetic-Grade Extraction" method (which effectively *is* the TPC extraction work we needed).
*   **She gets:** The "waste" biomass (skins/fibers) to experiment with *after* she extracts our data.

### Why this works:
1.  **Forces Technical Execution:** She can't make a serum without extracting it first. She *has* to do the lab work.
2.  **No Tutoring:** The requirement is "Cosmetic Grade," which is *her* specialization, so she must research it herself.
3.  **Aligns Interests:** She gets her "Zero Waste" project, but only as a byproduct of our main data collection.

---

## 4. Final Verdict
**Proceed with Option D.**
Send the "Green Extraction" counter-offer. If she accepts the technical responsibility of the extraction phase, she is hired. If she refuses to touch the wet lab work, she is rejected.
