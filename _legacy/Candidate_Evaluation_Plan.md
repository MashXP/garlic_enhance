# Personnel Evaluation: The "Green Extraction" Protocol

**Objective:** To verify if the candidate can translate her "Sustainability" vision into the concrete technical work required for Phase 1.

---

## 1. The Strategy: "The Anchor"
*   **The Problem:** The candidate wants to focus on "Strategy/Marketing" and avoid technical work.
*   **The Pivot:** We reframe the necessary "Wet Lab Extraction" work as the foundational step for her "Cosmetic/Zero-Waste" project.
*   **The Role:** **Green Extraction & Valorization Lead** (Trưởng nhóm Chiết xuất Xanh & Nâng cao Giá trị Sinh khối).

## 2. The Revised Task Assignment
*   **Context:** "We accept your vision for high-end cosmetics. However, this requires a strict **Green Chemistry** standard (No toxic residues)."
*   **The Challenge:**
    > "To proceed with the cosmetic angle, we need a **Green Extraction Protocol**. Please research and propose a method that:
    > 1.  **Safety:** Uses only food-grade/cosmetic-grade solvents (e.g., Ethanol vs. Water ratios). **No Methanol.**
    > 2.  **Efficiency:** Maximizes TPC yield (Antioxidants) without degrading the compounds (Temperature limit?).
    > 3.  **Zero-Waste Prep:** How do we treat the residue (skins/fiber) *during* extraction so it remains viable for your bioplastic experiments later?
    >
    > **Deliverable:** A 1-page 'Green Extraction Standard' we will use for the lab."

## 3. The "AI-Proof" Defense (Viva Voce)
*Crucial Step: Test if she understands the chemistry or just used ChatGPT.*

**The "Trap" Questions (Ask Live):**
1.  **The "Water Problem":**
    *   "You suggested 70% Ethanol for the cosmetic base. Fresh garlic is 60% water. If we just add 70% ethanol to fresh garlic, the final concentration drops to ~30%. How do you fix this?"
    *   *Passing Answer:* "Use 96-99% Ethanol to account for the water in the biomass."
    *   *Failing Answer:* Confusion or "It doesn't matter."
2.  **The "Oxidation" Issue:**
    *   "If we are making a high-end cosmetic ingredient, how do we prevent the garlic from turning blue/green (oxidation) during extraction?"
    *   *Passing Answer:* Mentions pH control, Vitamin C (Ascorbic acid), or limiting air exposure.
3.  **The "Waste" Reality:**
    *   "You want to use the residue for bioplastics. But if we boil it in ethanol, won't that denature the fibers? How do you balance extraction vs. fiber quality?"
    *   *Passing Answer:* "We keep the temperature below 60°C," or "We accept some degradation."

## 4. Evaluation Matrix (The Decision Rule)

| Metric | "Green Light" (Invite) | "Red Light" (Reject) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Technical Grit** | Accepts the extraction work as a necessary step. | Tries to negotiate out of the lab work again. |
| **Sustainability Logic** | Proposes specific "Green" solvents (e.g., Ethanol/Water, Glycerin). | Suggests standard toxic solvents (Methanol/Acetone) ignoring the "Cosmetic" constraint. |
| **Attention to Detail** | Accounts for "Fresh" garlic water content. | Copies a "Dry Powder" protocol blindly. |
| **Independence** | "I found this protocol for cosmetic extraction." | "Can Nghĩa teach me how to extract first?" |
